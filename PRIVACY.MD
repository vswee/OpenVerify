## 2. What OpenVerify Does

OpenVerify helps businesses verify whether a person appears to match a Trinidad and Tobago national identification card.

The service may check:

- Whether the uploaded ID card appears to match a supported Trinidad and Tobago ID card layout.
- Whether the ID card image is readable.
- Whether the selfie appears to match the photograph on the ID card.
- Whether extracted name or address information appears to match records in an electoral register database used by OpenVerify.
- Whether the approximate IP geolocation is close to the registered address associated with the matched electoral register record.
- Whether the verification should be approved automatically, rejected, marked for resubmission, or sent for manual review.

OpenVerify does not guarantee that a person is honest, trustworthy, creditworthy, eligible for a service, or free from fraud risk. It provides identity verification signals and a verification status. Businesses remain responsible for how they use that status.

## 3. Personal Information We Collect

Depending on the verification flow and the business customer’s configuration, we may collect the following information.

### 3.1 Information Provided by the Business Customer

A business customer may provide:

- Your external user or applicant reference.
- Your name.
- Your email address.
- Your phone number.
- The reason or purpose for verification.
- The deployment or onboarding flow where verification is required.

### 3.2 Information Provided by You

When you complete verification, we may collect:

- An image of the front of your Trinidad and Tobago national identification card.
- A selfie image.
- Consent confirmation.
- Any information you enter directly into the verification flow.

### 3.3 Information Extracted from Your ID Card

We may extract or infer information from your ID card image, including:

- Full name.
- ID number or other visible card identifiers.
- Date of birth, if visible and required.
- Address or address-related information, if visible and required.
- ID card version or layout type.
- ID card image quality and readability results.
- OCR confidence scores.

### 3.4 Biometric and Face Comparison Information

We may process your ID card photograph and selfie to compare whether they appear to show the same person.

This may include:

- Face detection results.
- Face match score.
- Face comparison provider result.
- Image quality checks.
- Cropped face image used for processing, if required.

We do not use your face image to create advertising profiles or unrelated biometric databases. The face comparison is used for identity verification only.

### 3.5 Electoral Register Match Information

Where available and configured, we may compare extracted identity information against an electoral register database used by OpenVerify.

This may include:

- Name match result.
- Address match result.
- Matched register record reference.
- Registered address.
- Approximate coordinates associated with the registered address.
- Match confidence scores.

### 3.6 Technical and Device Information

We may collect technical information such as:

- IP address.
- Approximate IP geolocation.
- Browser type and version.
- Device type.
- Operating system.
- User agent.
- Time and date of access.
- Session identifiers.
- Security logs.

### 3.7 Verification Results

We may generate and store:

- Verification session ID.
- Verification status.
- Overall validity score.
- Score breakdown.
- Decision reason.
- Manual review notes, if any.
- Review decision and reviewer identity.
- Webhook delivery records.
- Audit logs.

## 4. Why We Collect Personal Information

We collect and process personal information for these purposes:

1. To provide identity verification services requested by a business customer.
2. To confirm that a submitted ID card appears to match a supported Trinidad and Tobago ID card layout.
3. To check whether the ID card image is readable and suitable for review.
4. To compare a selfie against the photograph on the ID card.
5. To compare extracted identity information against available electoral register data.
6. To generate a verification score and status.
7. To send the verification result to the requesting business customer.
8. To allow manual review where automatic verification is uncertain.
9. To detect, prevent, and investigate suspected fraud, abuse, or misuse.
10. To secure the service and prevent unauthorised access.
11. To maintain audit logs and comply with legal, regulatory, contractual, and operational obligations.
12. To improve accuracy, reliability, and safety of the service, using data minimisation wherever possible.

We do not collect identity documents or selfies for unrelated marketing purposes.

## 6. How We Use Automated Decision-Making

OpenVerify uses automated checks to help decide whether a verification can be approved, rejected, marked for resubmission, or sent for manual review.

Automated checks may include:

- ID card layout confidence.
- OCR confidence.
- Face match score.
- Electoral register match confidence.
- IP geolocation proximity.
- Image quality and tamper-risk indicators.

Where the system is not confident, the verification may be marked as **needs review**. In that case, authorised reviewers may manually inspect submitted images, extracted data, score breakdowns, and related metadata.

A business customer may use the OpenVerify result as part of its own decision-making. OpenVerify does not control every decision made by the business customer after it receives a result.
